## The one customer, six modes
There is **one psychographic core**: a self-aware, guilt-carrying, therapy-literate
parent (overwhelmingly mothers) of a child aged 3–6 (purchase pull strongest 6–9).
She differentiates by **which battle owns her right now** — six modes:

| Segment | Her battle | Lead hook for our copy |
|---|---|---|
| **Healing Parent** (5th ICP, new) | Her own triggers, mom rage, breaking her cycle | "A second regulated nervous system in the room when yours is depleted." |
| **Routine Battler** | Mornings, bedtime, food, screens — daily friction | "The morning routine, minus the referee." |
| **Method Practitioner** (≈ Sturdy, May winner) | Runs the system; wants the mechanism | "Built with the scientists who wrote the science." Show the receipts. |
| **Social-Emotional Worrier** | Friendships, school, the child's outside world | "Recess is the test. Nowa is the practice." |
| **Dysregulation Firefighter** | Crisis: hitting, meltdowns, repair guilt | "A low-stakes place to practice calm — before the storm, not during it." |
| **Community Curator** | Finds, vets, shares — the evangelist | Give her a defensible recommendation + a comparison chart. |

**Nostalgia Bridge Dad** is a separate track (won on CPM in May, but absent from the
parenting-community data). He buys a Tamagotchi memory, not a philosophy — nostalgia
visual language, spec + price clarity, gift framing. **Do not** use the guilt-repair
register on him.

## The universal emotional spine
Across every mode, the pain ladder bottoms out at the same identity-level fear:
**"I keep yelling, and I'm becoming the parent I swore I wouldn't be."**
The aspiration mirrors it: staying calm — and the child showing self-regulation
*unprompted* (the most-celebrated moment in 16.5K community posts).

## Two language registers (use the right one per surface)
- **Cold register** (pre-conversion, for ads + SEO titles): plain problem language —
  "how to stop yelling at my kids," "toddler won't brush teeth," "child meltdowns when to worry."
- **Warm register** (post-arrival, for landing pages + blog + community): the reframe
  language — calm, regulation, repair, "good kid having a hard time," practice not fixing.

## What this means for the website
- The site is the **destination** ads and social point to — it must carry the warm register and convert.
- Lead pillar by segment varies; the universal spine (guilt → repair, calm, unprompted regulation) is the emotional throughline of the whole site.
- Organic search is the #1 channel for most peer brands — the blog/SEO engine is a primary acquisition asset, not an afterthought.
- The "win moment" (child regulates unprompted) is our highest-resonance story — feature it everywhere.
